# 42 image-generation art direction

The service imagery was generated as a single editorial family with OpenAI's built-in image-generation workflow. Final assets are stored as 1440 × 960 WebP files in `public/images/services/`.

## Shared prompt direction

> Create a premium landscape 3:2 website service-card image as a high-end contemporary editorial still life. Express a business system through physically believable architectural model-making on a warm off-white technical surface. Use near-black ink forms, warm paper, a restrained signal-green decision marker, and at most one small cobalt-blue connector. Use tactile matte paper, blackened or anodized metal, smoked or clear acrylic, fine grids, and soft directional gallery lighting. Keep the composition sophisticated, crop-safe, original, calm, and technically credible. No people, text, letters, numbers, logos, brand marks, screens, recognizable software UI, watermarks, generic 3D blobs, glassmorphism, neon cyberpunk, stock-office imagery, clip-art icons, or cheap CGI.

## Service-specific subjects

| Service | Subject prompt |
| --- | --- |
| Strategy and consulting | An architectural planning table with layered drafting sheets, dark geometric models, a green decision marker, measured grid lines, and a restrained blue annotation. |
| Implementation and onboarding | An ordered assembly of modular dark blocks being fitted into a precise paper framework, with a newly placed green module and a blue alignment rail. |
| CRM and RevOps | A network of dark data tiles and metal channels converging into one ordered pipeline, with a green record token and a restrained blue cross-connection. |
| Automation and operations | A precise sequence of mechanical gates and tracks moving repeated paper tokens, controlled by a green switch and a subtle blue connector. |
| Integrations and development | Two distinct dark physical systems connected by a clear acrylic bridge, a blue conduit, and a green synchronization token. |
| Websites and Content Hub | A modular publication layout built from layered paper panels, image windows, and a typographic grid, linked by a blue journey line and green conversion marker. |
| Service Hub | A central dark support-desk sculpture receiving paper routes and returning resolved green tokens, with one blue priority route. |
| Managed support | A precision maintenance bench with a stable modular system, ordered paper backlog tiles, abstract calibration tools, a green indicator, and a blue measurement line. |

These images are conceptual service illustrations. They do not depict real clients, client systems, employees, outcomes, or HubSpot interface elements.
