# 42 — HubSpot Consultancy Website Master Brief

**Document purpose:** This is the source-of-truth brief for designing and building the first full website for **42**, a senior-led HubSpot consultancy.

**Primary implementation tools:** Codex, Figma, GitHub, and a modern React-based deployment platform.

**Working brand line:** **42. Your HubSpot answer.**

---

## 1. Executive Direction

Build a premium, distinctive, highly credible website that positions **42** as a world-class HubSpot consultancy with senior strategic thinking, hands-on technical execution, and unusually clear communication.

The website must not look like:

- a generic digital marketing agency;
- a HubSpot template with orange gradients and stock illustrations;
- a solo freelancer portfolio;
- a science-fiction fan site;
- an imitation of any competitor or reference template.

The website should feel like:

- a senior consultancy with deep HubSpot and systems expertise;
- a confident specialist that can diagnose complex business problems;
- a technically capable team that can also communicate clearly with non-technical stakeholders;
- a modern, editorial, design-led consultancy;
- a company that simplifies complexity without oversimplifying the work.

The central brand idea is derived from the cultural association of **42 as “the answer”**, but the brand must stand independently. References to *The Hitchhiker’s Guide to the Galaxy* should be subtle and occasional. Do not use copied artwork, book-cover motifs, character references, long quotations, or visual assets that could imply an official association.

## 2. Strategic Positioning

### 2.1 Brand

**Name:** 42  
**Category descriptor:** HubSpot consultancy  
**Primary tagline:** Your HubSpot answer.  
**Alternative supporting line:** Clear answers for complex HubSpot problems.

### 2.2 Core positioning statement

> 42 is a senior-led HubSpot consultancy that helps growing businesses design, build, connect, and improve the systems behind their customer journey.

### 2.3 Homepage value proposition

> HubSpot is powerful, but only when it is structured around the way your business actually works. 42 combines strategy, CRM architecture, automation, websites, integrations, reporting, and enablement to turn HubSpot into a system your team can trust.

### 2.4 The commercial problem 42 solves

Prospective clients often have one or more of these problems:

- HubSpot was purchased but never implemented properly.
- The CRM has become cluttered, inconsistent, or difficult to trust.
- Sales, marketing, service, and operations work in separate processes.
- Reporting cannot answer basic commercial questions.
- Teams are doing manual work that should be automated.
- Existing workflows fire unpredictably or conflict with one another.
- HubSpot does not reflect the actual business process.
- Legacy systems, ERPs, finance platforms, or operational tools are disconnected.
- The business needs custom development rather than another workaround.
- The internal team needs senior guidance and hands-on delivery, not just recommendations.
- The company has outgrown onboarding but does not need a large retained agency.

### 2.5 The answer 42 provides

42 delivers:

1. **Clarity** — understand the real business problem before configuring the platform.
2. **Architecture** — design data, objects, pipelines, permissions, processes, and integrations coherently.
3. **Execution** — configure and build the system, rather than handing over a strategy deck.
4. **Enablement** — document, train, test, and hand over work so teams can operate confidently.
5. **Improvement** — continue optimising the platform as the business changes.

### 2.6 Differentiators

Use these throughout the website:

- **Senior-led from discovery to delivery**  
  Clients work directly with experienced consultants and technical specialists.

- **Strategy and implementation together**  
  42 does not stop at advice. The team maps, configures, builds, tests, and improves.

- **Technical depth beyond standard HubSpot onboarding**  
  Custom objects, APIs, middleware, CRM architecture, custom modules, automation, data migration, and connected systems are within scope.

- **Business process before platform configuration**  
  HubSpot is designed around the operating model, not the other way around.

- **Clear documentation and handover**  
  Every system should remain understandable after implementation.

- **Practical simplicity**  
  42 removes avoidable complexity while preserving the sophistication the business genuinely needs.

### 2.7 Positioning to avoid

Do not lead with broad claims such as:

- “full-service digital agency”;
- “we transform businesses”;
- “growth hacking”;
- “end-to-end digital transformation” without specific evidence;
- “award-winning” unless verified;
- “HubSpot Elite/Platinum/Diamond Partner” unless verified and supplied;
- invented client counts, certifications, review counts, revenue impact, or performance metrics.

- Companies moving from spreadsheets or legacy CRMs

The number 42 is widely recognised as “the answer.” For this consultancy, it represents something more practical: the point where business questions, customer processes, data, technology, and execution meet.

HubSpot often gives companies more capability than clarity. 42 exists to turn that capability into an answer that works.

### 5.2 Narrative themes

- Questions and answers
- Complexity and clarity
- Systems and people
- Strategy and execution
- Connected journeys
- Navigation and guidance
- Practical intelligence
- Confidence after uncertainty

### 5.3 Subtle reference rules

Allowed:

- “The answer is 42.”
- “Find your HubSpot answer.”
- “A practical guide to your HubSpot universe.”
- “No panic. Just a plan.”
- A very subtle star-grid, coordinate, orbital-line, or guide-interface detail.
- A tasteful 404 message involving being “lost in the digital universe.”
- Tiny easter eggs such as the number 42 in animation timings, labels, or coordinates.

Avoid:

- copying *The Hitchhiker’s Guide to the Galaxy* cover design;
- the green planet logo or close variants;
- direct character, spaceship, towel, dolphin, or book references;
- repeated “Don’t Panic” use;
- long quotations;
- making the cultural reference the main sales message;
- anything that makes the consultancy feel novelty-led.

## 21. Motion and Interaction Specification

### Motion philosophy

Motion should make the website feel engineered and premium. It must not delay access to content or make the interface difficult to use.

### Required motion patterns

- Hero text and image entrance
- Image mask reveals
- Staggered card entrances
- Scroll-triggered section reveals
- Sticky process sequence
- Subtle parallax on selected editorial images
- Animated connector lines in system diagrams
- Number or metric reveal for verified proof
- Header surface transition on scroll
- Smooth mobile menu
- Hover states with depth and directional movement
- Page transition kept short and non-blocking

### Recommended implementation

- Motion library for component-level transitions
- CSS transitions for simple hover/focus effects
- Intersection Observer or motion viewport triggers
- Avoid global smooth-scrolling libraries unless the implementation remains accessible and stable
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`
- No animation should be required to understand content
- No scroll hijacking
- No long intro loader
- Avoid animating large blur filters on mobile
- Avoid excessive simultaneous animations

### Timing direction

- Micro-interactions: 120–220 ms
- Component entrance: 350–650 ms
- Section sequences: 600–1,000 ms total
- Use 42 as an occasional timing easter egg only when it does not compromise UX, such as 420 ms.
